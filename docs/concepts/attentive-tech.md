## What is Attentive Tech?

**Attentive tech preserves and supports the user's capacity to consciously allocate their own attention, rather than extracting or hijacking it.**

### The Problem It Solves

Attention is the scarcest resource humans possess. The modern digital landscape runs on an extraction economyâ€”every app, platform, and notification competes to capture and monetize your consciousness.

Most technology is an **upstream allocator**: it fills your attention slots before you arrive (feeds, recommendations, notifications). You react to what algorithms have already chosen for you.

Attentive tech is a **downstream allocator**: you consciously fill your own attention slots. The technology makes this allocation visible and manipulable, but you remain sovereign.

## Five Core Principles

### 1. Downstream Allocation
- User fills attention slots; technology doesn't pre-fill them
- No algorithmic feeds, no automated recommendations
- Allocation decisions are explicit and visible, not invisible and automated

### 2. Infrastructure, Not Experience
- Boring by designâ€”stable, reliable, unsexy
- **Multi-interface**: screens, e-ink, physical objects, wearables, voice, tactile
- Peripheral presence (accessible when needed, invisible when not)
- Serves focal practices rather than becoming the focus itself
- Calm technology principles: multiple sensory channels, ambient awareness
- "The best tool is no tool" - disappears into background when working well

### 3. Anti-Measurement (with nuance)
- No extractive tracking that demands engagement
- **Attentive measurement**: passive, ambient devices (wearables, APIs, sensors)
- Peripheral observation without interruption (SuperNote semantic extraction example)
- Measurement that respects focal practices rather than commodifying them
- Strategic friction: addictive products should be harder to access, intentional ones easier

### 4. Convivial Constraints
- **Accessible**: Simple, comprehensible, no expertise required
- **Adaptable**: User can modify, configure, appropriate for their needs
- **Decentralized**: No central authority controlling the experience
  - Local-first architecture
  - P2P federation over platform control
  - Open source, forkable, modifiable
- **Human-scale**: Manageable, graspable, not overwhelming
- **Holistic**: User maintains sovereignty over how they use it

### 5. Consciousness as Currency
- Treats attention as precious and finite
- Respects the scarcity of focused attention
- Supports conscious choice over compulsive consumption

## The Four-Test Framework

Technology is attentive if it passes these tests:

1. **Franklin**: Is it holistic or prescriptive?  
   (User maintains control vs. system dictates behavior)

2. **Illich**: Does it enhance autonomy or create dependency?  
   (Can you stop using it without losing capability?)

3. **Winner**: What social order does it encode?  
   (Who has powerâ€”user or platform?)

4. **Borgmann**: Does it enable engagement or optimize convenience?  
   (Focal practice vs. device consumption)

## Why "Attentive" Over "Mindful"?

### Mindful is Captured
"Mindfulness" has been co-opted by the attention economy:
- Meditation apps with streak counters
- Wellness services that quantify presence
- The term now signals commodity, not paradigm

### Attentive is Precise
- Names the exact resource: **attention**
- More active: you *attend to* (verb) vs. *being mindful* (state)
- Direct opposition to "attention economy" and "extractive tech"
- Makes political stakes clear: sovereignty over consciousness

### Attentive Creates a Category
**"Mindful tech"** = competing with meditation apps in wellness space  
**"Attentive tech"** = defining a new infrastructure category

Not self-help. Infrastructure for attention sovereignty.

### Attentive is Clearer
- "Mindful technology" = tech that is mindful (anthropomorphic, confusing)
- "Attentive technology" = tech that helps you attend (clear directionality)

### Attentive Has Less Baggage
"Mindful" carries spiritual/wellness associations that can alienate:
- Engineers who want tools that respect attention without wellness framing
- Founders focused on productivity, not meditation
- People allergic to self-help language

"Attentive" is neutral, accessible, focused on the problem.

- **Notification-heavy tools**: Interruption as feature
- **Recommender systems**: Fill attention slots algorithmically
